# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is accessibility auditors, designers, QA engineers, and developers who need to inspect interfaces in the state people actually use them. A secondary audience includes clients and product teams opening shared reports to understand a barrier and act on it.

## Product Purpose

TheWCAG is a companion website for a local-first macOS and Windows accessibility workspace. It explains the desktop tools, distributes signed application builds, provides practical accessibility guidance, handles passwordless accounts and desktop authorization, and presents annotated reports without losing their context. The standalone Screenshot tool must remain useful to people who keep their audit record in another product or process.

Success means a visitor quickly understands why a desktop inspection tool is necessary, chooses the right application download, and can trust the report, account, and verification flows.

## Positioning

TheWCAG inspects the rendered experience anywhere on screen, then keeps the barrier, evidence, WCAG context, and handoff together.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Download the desktop application.
- Secondary CTA: Explore how the audit workflow works.
- Standalone activation path: Capture, annotate, copy, export, and share a screenshot without creating or adopting a TheWCAG audit.
- Traceability promise: every captured issue and finding gets one immutable global ID at creation; short audit sequence numbers remain display references only.
- The line to remember: Audit what people see.
- Belief ladder: accessibility barriers happen beyond source code; TheWCAG can inspect any rendered interface; it keeps evidence connected to findings; captures remain local until the auditor publishes; the application supports both macOS and Windows.
- Proof on hand: the working desktop tools, documented WCAG 2.2 capabilities, signed release pipeline, public report format, source repository, and product behavior described in the repository. No invented customer logos, usage numbers, certifications, or testimonials.

## Brand Personality

Precise, observant, accountable. The experience should feel like a calibrated inspection instrument paired with a clear evidence sheet: confident and human, but never cute, performative, or overdesigned.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS card walls, oversized centered slogans, fake product screenshots, decorative diagrams, glassmorphism, gradient text, playful accessibility imagery, timid gray-on-gray interfaces, and motion added only for spectacle.

## Design Principles

1. Product evidence carries the story.
2. Every screen should make the next action obvious.
3. Accessibility claims must be visible in the interaction details.
4. Dense audit information should remain calm and legible.
5. Preserve user control, especially around local captures, publishing, authentication, and deletion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 Level AA across the website and supporting product surfaces. Maintain semantic landmarks and heading order, visible keyboard focus, 44px interaction targets where possible, labeled controls, reflow at 320px, high zoom support, forced-colors resilience, reduced-motion support, and status or error communication that never relies on color alone.
